# ECONOMICS

## What a converted lead is worth to Credex

Credex sells discounted AI infrastructure credits. Assume:
- Average credit purchase: **$2,000** (one-time or recurring quarterly)
- Gross margin on credits: **20%** (discounted credits have thin margins; the value is volume and repeat purchases)
- Gross profit per transaction: **$400**
- Average customer makes **2 purchases/year**: **$800 gross profit/year**
- Customer stays for **2 years** on average: **LTV ≈ $1,600**

This is conservative. Enterprise customers purchasing $10k+ credits would have significantly higher LTV. Using $1,600 as the baseline LTV.

## CAC at each GTM channel

| Channel | Effort | Estimated signups | Consultation rate | Purchases | Blended CAC |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| HN Show HN | 2 hrs to write + respond | 30 signups | 10% → 3 consultations | 1.5 purchases | $0 cash / ~$40 time |
| Reddit posts | 1 hr/post × 2 posts | 40 signups | 8% → 3 consultations | 1.5 purchases | $0 cash |
| Cold DMs (X/LinkedIn) | 3 hrs | 15 signups | 15% → 2 consultations | 1 purchase | $0 cash |
| Lenny's Slack | 30 min | 25 signups | 12% → 3 consultations | 1.5 purchases | $0 cash / $20 membership |
| Product Hunt | 1 day | 50 signups | 5% → 2 consultations | 1 purchase | $0 cash |

**Total month 1:** ~160 audits, ~14 consultations booked, ~7 credit purchases, $0 paid CAC

At $1,600 LTV and $0 paid CAC, the economics are trivially positive — the constraint is time and distribution, not money.

At scale with paid distribution:
- LinkedIn ads to engineering managers: estimated CPL = $80–$120
- At 20% audit → consultation rate and 50% consultation → purchase: CAC ≈ $200–$300
- At $1,600 LTV: LTV/CAC ≈ 5–8x. Healthy for a B2B SaaS-adjacent model.

## Conversion funnel

```
Landing page visitors
    → Audit started:           40% (good landing page + free, no friction)
    → Audit completed:         70% (form is short, value is clear)
    → Email captured:          30% (post-value gate, high intent)
    → Consultation booked:     10% of email captures (high-savings cases only)
    → Credit purchase:         50% of consultations
```

So: **100 visitors → 40 audits → 28 completions → 8 emails → 0.8 consultations → 0.4 purchases**

To hit $1M ARR in 18 months from this tool alone, you need ~625 credit purchases at $2,000 each (assuming some churn and mix of purchase sizes). At the 0.4 purchases per 100 visitors rate, you need **156,000 unique visitors** over 18 months — roughly 8,700/month.

8,700 monthly visitors is achievable with a combination of organic (SEO on "AI tool cost" queries), earned (HN, Reddit, Product Hunt), and Credex's existing network. It requires consistent distribution work but no extraordinary paid budget.

## What would have to be true for $1M ARR in 18 months

1. **Average credit deal size rises to $3,000+** — likely as Credex adds enterprise clients and larger inventory. This alone reduces the visitor requirement by 33%.

2. **Retention improves** — if customers purchase 3x/year instead of 2x, LTV = $2,400 and the math gets significantly easier.

3. **The tool becomes a benchmark product** — if SpendLens gets cited in industry newsletters and blog posts (passive SEO + authority), organic traffic compounds. 10 high-quality backlinks could drive 500+ monthly visitors with no ongoing effort.

4. **Consultation → purchase rate hits 60%+** — requires Credex to have enough inventory breadth that they can serve most high-savings audit customers. If the supply is there, this conversion rate is very achievable because the audit pre-qualifies intent.

5. **Referral loop works** — users who share their audit URL drive new visitors. At 30% of users sharing and each share generating 2 new visitors, there's a 0.6 referral multiplier on top of direct traffic.

**Summary:** The tool is profitable at almost any visitor volume (near-zero marginal cost per audit). The $1M ARR scenario is plausible but requires Credex to treat distribution as a first-class priority for 18 months, not a side project.
